Introduction: AI-Driven Cybersecurity Patent Landscape

Artificial intelligence is becoming a core layer of modern cybersecurity systems. Traditional rule-based security tools are increasingly being supplemented by machine learning, behavioral analytics, automated threat detection, adaptive response systems, and AI-assisted vulnerability management. This landscape examines how patent activity reflects that shift.

The focus of this patent landscape is on inventions where AI, machine learning, analytics, automation, or intelligent decision-making are applied to cybersecurity problems. These include threat detection, network and cloud protection, identity security, malware and phishing defense, AI model security, privacy protection, security testing, and risk management.

The purpose of this landscape is to identify the main technology directions, leading patent filers, category-wise concentration of innovation, filing and publication trends, important patents, problem areas being addressed, and possible ideation opportunities. The analysis is based on the patent dataset provided in the Excel file and uses a consistent 8-category framework throughout the deck.

Introduction

Cybersecurity systems are moving from static, rule-based defense toward adaptive, AI-assisted security architectures. AI is being used to detect unknown threats, prioritize alerts, identify vulnerabilities, monitor user behavior, protect cloud and network infrastructure, defend against malware and phishing, and secure AI models themselves.

This patent landscape captures that transition by analyzing patent activity in AI-driven cybersecurity. It identifies where companies are filing, which technology areas are most active, how innovation is distributed across categories, and what problems are repeatedly being addressed. The landscape also highlights possible future ideation areas based on gaps and emerging technical directions in the dataset.
